Marigold - Symbol of Warmth

Marigolds are known for their vibrant orange and yellow hues, symbolizing warmth, creativity, and passion. These flowers are widely used in ceremonies and festivals across various cultures, especially in South Asia and Latin America. In India, marigolds are often used in religious rituals and garlands, symbolizing devotion. In Mexico, they are associated with the Day of the Dead, where they guide spirits back to the living world. Marigolds are also known for their pest-repelling properties, making them popular in gardens. Their resilience and bright color represent the power of the sun and the cycle of life and death.
